CMS VS CUSTOM DEVELOPMENT:


By Team TriosCo | January 25, 2023 - 5 mins read

Are you choosing the right website development framework?


You might need a coffee for this one!
You’ve overcome the initial procrastination and you are finally geared up for your business. You need a website and you do not know what to do! There are two potential options you need to choose from - The CMS (content management system) and custom web development. But let’s be honest, most of us don’t get it!

According to IDC, 60% of market leaders will have systemic, structured digital innovation plans and investments in place by 2027. Without going digital in the 21st century, growth, scale, agility, and resilience are next to impossible. But what should be the right choice for a business when it comes to a website? It depends on four major questions:

1. Are you looking for customization?
2. Is UI/UX and Performance at the top of your priority list?
3. Is Scalability and Security of website your major concern?
4. What is the Time and Cost of your website development?
5. How complex is your website going to be?



WEBSITE DEVELOPMENT IN CMS V/S CUSTOM CODE


Let’s help you decide.


Design

We are not unacquainted with the immense popularity of CMS or content management systems when it comes to business needs. Laymen can opt to use these platforms to create new websites, let alone citizen developers or experienced ones. CMS has an upper hand, given its ease of usability, functionality, and cost of development. But, if you want to meet your long-term demands within your website and create designs based on the specifications of individual projects, custom code is the option you should be going for.


Easy Deployment

CMS assists firms in gaining early adopters, validating and designing ideas, ensuring seamless integration throughout the lifecycle, and ensuring speedier development and delivery. Custom development projects, alternatively, necessitate prior effort in order to achieve an effective framework and put an appropriate design for the target audience in their business.

If you want to launch a web resource, a custom code website takes about 120-1000 hours on average. On the other hand, with prototypes and preset templates already in place, CMS is a time saver, if you’re on the clock.


Security


If security is a primary priority for your brand, custom-built websites outperform CMS platforms in general. CMS frameworks rely mostly on third-party security, but a custom coded site is built from the ground up. So, security is only in your hand. However, if your website does not have highly confidential information, most CMS infrastructures work out just fine.


Maintenance


The CMS infrastructure helps you navigate your site easily as the interface is super user-friendly. So most often, if your website needs some minor changes and content update, you can just choose CMS. If you’re not able to get a hang of the website, most often a customer service call will fix it. That being said, custom-coded frameworks are not entirely difficult but a tad bit cumbersome for citizen developers to maintain while catering to website development. However, there’s a bright side, given you know to work around your websites or a dedicated team of developers, it won’t need any third-party interjection.


Scalability


Cross-platform functionality is important in today’s day and age as most E-commerce plug-ins are next to impossible on CMS framework. Scalability will often not be a big problem for you, as most CMS platforms like Shopify, Webflow, WordPress web development services etc. allow minor plug-ins if that’s what you’re looking for. But if API integrations and major plug-ins are required for your business, then adopting a CMS framework may restrict you to the infrastructure of the platform and make it difficult to increase the site's features.


While each has its own pros and cons, for a long, durable, and secured point-to-point integration, custom development is recommended. While it is not untrue that the initially a custom website development prices are higher than CMS, it’s always more accessible, customized, and cost-efficient in the long run. However, if you are looking for flexibility, maintenance, and a budget-friendly website development cost, CMS can be a fair choice.
