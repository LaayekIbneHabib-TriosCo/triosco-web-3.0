AMAZON CREATED THE GAME


By Author Name | January 23, 2023 - 2 mins read

When we say the word ‘eCommerce’ what comes to mind? Is it the world of online selling? Perhaps you think of a marketplace? Or maybe you’re like us and the thing that comes to mind is Amazon.


THE JURASSIC ERA OF ECOMMERCE


Online marketplaces date all the way back to the 90s, specifically 1995. And while you may not think that was a long time ago, it’s actually been 27 years, which is 100 lifetimes in the tech world. So, you can imagine, the realm of eComm was vastly different to what we know it today. When the internet was conceived, numerous tech companies came up with their homogenous or unique offering of consumer products and services via the internet. In other words, these companies attempted to create an online economy for the Americans of the internet age. Such dot com companies garnered the attention of the investors who bid massive amounts of money into such companies with the notion of them being the future of the internet. And, as expected, the valuation grew their market worth over and above their actual value and this resulted in an outrageous increase in their stock prices which eventually collapsed in a deep dive domino effect. This market crash became known as the dot com bubble. But you see, at the end of the day, these companies were selling hope. Nothing more and nothing less. Think Theranos but late 90s early 2000s. The idea was great, the dream was one for the history books, but the implementation was an epic failure. However, through all of this, really one company managed to really rise out of the ashes to become the biggest eCommerce company on our green earth, and I'm sure you guessed it. Amazon.

WILL AMAZON FOREVER REMAIN RELEVANT?


Let’s quickly go over Amazon’s founding story. It was founded in 1995, in a garage, with the initial idea being an online marketplace for books. Jump to 2000, Amazon changed its business model and logo to become the world's first and leading online marketplace to sell any product from A-Z with a smile. So, you see, amazon didn’t change the game, it created it. It truly is an accomplishment for one company, to not only continuously be the industry leader, but to also innovate it. It’s a skill which Amazon has mastered over the years. At the end of the day, Amazon has always had one main ethos in mind, Innovate, Create, and Transend. Furthermore, Amazon has its eggs in a lot of baskets to generate its revenue, which essentially means it’s more than just an eCommerce platform. In addition to the eCommerce platform, it also has Amazon Prime, Amazon Music, Audible, Kindle, and Alexa. And that’s just for us consumers. They also have AWS and Amazon Robotics that hold a big share of the B2B market in their respective domains. So, to answer to question in short, yes Amazon will always remain relevant in our books.

WHAT WILL THE FUTURE OF AMAZON LOOK LIKE?


In a future where AI is geared towards helping us have a better quality of life, Amazon’s investments in Ring, eCommerce, Prime logistics, Prime videos, Prime Music, Audible, Kindle, and Alexa secure their existence as a company set to defend and grow in a dynamic market that is unpredictable and evolving with every alternate day. Amazon’s portfolio of assets best places it in a position of being able to leverage its massive database of consumer purchase behavior to make game-changing predictions by implementing AI ML in a way that humans, so far, were unable to comprehend. Amazons lead in such data centric decision making ensures that it never misses out on spearheading the next big bubble.

CONCLUSION


Ecommerce was a game changer in shopping, but Amazon created the game that so many dreamed of inventing. Bezos took his vision and implemented it in a way that would be able to grow in more ways than one, even without him at the fore front, ensuring that Amazon will forever be the industry leader.
