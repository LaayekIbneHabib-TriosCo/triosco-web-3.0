CRACK THE COLOR CODE

By Team TriosCo | January 23, 2023 - 5 mins read

Colors are everywhere. They affect our emotions and behaviors. Choosing the right colors for your brand identity can trigger the desired action from a consumer.

If you’re looking to make a strong brand identity, this is it! Do you know that the most common favorite color is blue? If no, you’re welcome and if yes, there’s more you can discover.

You see, color psychology in logo design is a fascinating little enigma. Every color has a meaning, a personality, a theme, and a feeling. Let’s take a simple example. Has it ever occurred to you why surgeons wear blue or green scrubs? It’s because it soothes the eye, and lessens emotional fatigue. In short, it refreshes their vision while they constantly see red in the operating rooms.

More often than not, blue and green are used as de-stressing colors in the world of psychology. In the center of every big brand identity, there is a recognizable logo with a suitable color. When we say McDonald’s, you already have the huge arches of M in yellow against the red backdrop right in your mind. You’re visualizing it! This is the power of color.

BRAND IT LIKE YOU OWN IT


Additionally, to capture the attention of your target audience, a color scheme is step one. Whether you are branding or rebranding, you have to do it strategically. Moreover, all your favorite brands became giants with well-designed logos. The color, typeface, and shapes are used to trigger the emotions of their customers. Henceforth, it encourages them to buy the products.

  • Let’s take a look, shall we?

    Red: Coca-Cola
    A bold beverage company utilized the powerful effect of bright red in its logo. What's more, they became a huge success with a presence in 200+ countries. An amazing feat from the first-ever Olympic sponsor! 

  • Blue: Facebook/Meta
    A giant tech company that gained billions of active users. Furthermore, their logo has the most common favorite color – blue. Additionally, it's the founder’s favorite color as well. Consecutively, Facebook is now the 7th most valuable brand in the world! Blue builds trust.

  • Yellow: McDonald’s
    A multinational fast-food chain with a recognizable logo used yellow & red. Moreover, these are the colors that make people feel hungry. Now, over 50 million people eat at McDonald's every day. 

  • Green: Tropicana
    Yes, we are talking about the delicious juice producer you know and love. Moreover, they have smartly used green in their logo color to invoke feelings of freshness and health. Also, recent market analysis has announced their revenue as $75.8 Million.

  • Orange: JBL
    An international audio equipment manufacturer used the orange color in their logo. Furthermore, it indicates energy & passion, which played a big role in bringing music to life for over 70 years.

  • Purple: Cadbury
    A luxurious chocolate maker who used the richness of purple color in their logo. They embraced it so effectively that now their factories make 250,000+ Dairy Milk Buttons/min. Also, that’s 15 million/hour & 400 million/day. That’s a LOT!

  • Pink: Cosmopolitan
    The Cosmo team works to deliver juicy content to you. They used the feminine and playful interpretation of pink in their logo. The result? gaining 64 international editions of their magazines that are mostly made for women.

  • Black: Apple
    A tech giant used the trendy dominance of black color in their logo. Now, became the world's first $3 trillion company! No one could have foreseen the growth of what started in Steve Jobs’ parents' garage. All the minimalist fellas out there, this premium color is for you!

HOW TO PICK YOUR BRAND’S COLOR:


Take a pause before you start the process of branding or rebranding. Think about the message you want your brand identity to convey. What particulars do you want to highlight and put under the spotlight? Are you speedy like postal delivery? innovative like the IT companies? compassionate like a non-profit non-governmental community? Consecutively, brand identity aspects that your audience finds appealing must be at the top of this list. In addition, colors help consumers categorize businesses, their products, and their services. Also, they can identify which are more to their liking. Do you know what you want your brand identity to signify? Great! you can start thinking of the color characteristics.

IS THAT IT OR THERE'S MORE TO THE BRAND STORY?


There are two other things we cannot forget. Color psychology in good logo designs also has cultural associations and industry trends. Cultural associations happen when certain communities prefer a particular type of style. Let us break it down for you. Here's an example of color and culture. In the West, people wear black for funerals. Whereas, in some Asian and Middle-Eastern cultures, people wear white. Polar opposite choices in three intertwining cultures!
